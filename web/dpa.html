Data Processing Agreement

Version 1.0 · Effective date: 1 July 2026 · Governed by GDPR Article 28 & UK GDPR
Between Insight IT Solutions Ltd ("Processor") and the Customer ("Controller")
This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") forms part of, and is incorporated into, the PrismRAG Terms of Service or any Order Form entered into between the parties. In the event of conflict, this DPA takes precedence with respect to data protection matters.

1. Definitions

Terms used but not defined here have the meanings given in the applicable data-protection legislation (GDPR, UK GDPR, Swiss FADP).

  • "Personal Data" — any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person processed under this DPA.
  • "Controller" — the Customer, who determines the purposes and means of processing.
  • "Processor" — Insight IT Solutions Ltd, trading as PrismRAG, processing on behalf of the Controller.
  • "Sub-processor" — any third party engaged by the Processor to process Personal Data.
  • "Services" — the PrismRAG semantic re-mapping engine, Deliberation engine, and related APIs provided by the Processor.

3. Controller Instructions

The Processor shall process Personal Data only on the documented instructions of the Controller, unless required to do otherwise by applicable law. The Services themselves, as configured by the Controller, constitute the Controller's documented instructions. The Processor shall promptly notify the Controller if it believes an instruction would infringe applicable data-protection law.

4. Processor Obligations

The Processor agrees to:

  1. Process Personal Data only as necessary to provide the Services or comply with applicable law.
  2. Ensure that persons authorised to process Personal Data are bound by appropriate confidentiality obligations.
  3. Implement and maintain appropriate technical and organisational security measures as described in Section 7.
  4. Respect the conditions for engaging Sub-processors set out in Section 5.
  5. Assist the Controller in responding to requests from data subjects exercising their rights (Articles 15–22 GDPR) within the timescales required by law.
  6. Assist the Controller in complying with its obligations under Articles 32–36 GDPR (security, breach notification, DPIA, prior consultation), taking into account the nature of processing and information available.
  7. At the choice of the Controller, delete or return all Personal Data to the Controller on termination of the Services, and delete existing copies unless storage is required by applicable law.
  8. Make available to the Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA and allow for and contribute to audits conducted by the Controller or a mandated auditor (on reasonable notice, no more than once per year, at Controller's cost).

6. International Transfers

Where Personal Data is transferred outside the European Economic Area or UK, the Processor will ensure that one of the following safeguards applies:

  • An adequacy decision of the European Commission or UK Secretary of State;
  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission or UK ICO; or
  • Another valid transfer mechanism under applicable law.

On request, the Processor shall make available the relevant SCCs or other transfer documentation. The Processor incorporates the EU SCCs (Module 2 — Controller to Processor) by reference into this DPA where applicable.

7. Security Measures

The Processor maintains the following technical and organisational measures (TOMs):

  • Encryption at rest: AES-256 encryption for all stored data via Azure and Neon.
  • Encryption in transit: TLS 1.2+ enforced on all endpoints.
  • Access control: Role-based access control (RBAC); MFA available and enforceable at tenant level.
  • Audit logging: All data-access and administrative actions logged with timestamps and user identity.
  • Penetration testing: Annual third-party penetration test; results shared with Controllers on request.
  • Vulnerability management: Container images scanned on build; critical CVEs patched within 72 hours.
  • Incident response: Documented incident response plan; sub-72-hour notification to affected Controllers for high-risk breaches.
  • Backups: Daily encrypted backups with 7-day retention; tested quarterly.
  • Data isolation: Logical tenant isolation enforced via row-level security on all tables.
  • Customer-managed encryption keys (CMEK): Available on Enterprise tier via Azure Key Vault integration.

8. Personal Data Breach Notification

In the event of a Personal Data Breach (as defined under applicable data-protection legislation), the Processor shall:

  1. Notify the Controller without undue delay, and in any event within 48 hours of becoming aware of a breach that is likely to result in a risk to natural persons.
  2. Provide the Controller with sufficient information to enable it to comply with its own notification obligations to supervisory authorities and data subjects, including: the nature of the breach; categories and approximate number of data subjects and records concerned; likely consequences; and measures taken or proposed.
  3. Cooperate with and assist the Controller in all aspects of breach notification and remediation.

9. Data Subject Rights

The Controller is responsible for responding to data subjects' requests. The Processor shall assist the Controller by:

  • Promptly forwarding any data-subject request received directly by the Processor;
  • Providing technical tools (e.g. tenant data export, deletion APIs) to help the Controller fulfil requests; and
  • Completing Controller-requested deletions or exports within 30 days.

10. Deletion and Return of Data

On expiry or termination of the Services, the Processor shall, at the Controller's written election:

  • Delete all Personal Data processed on behalf of the Controller, including all copies, within 30 days of termination; or
  • Return all Personal Data in a machine-readable format (JSONL or CSV) within 30 days.

The Processor may retain Personal Data for a longer period only to the extent required by applicable law, and shall promptly inform the Controller of any such legal obligation.

11. Confidentiality

The Processor shall keep Personal Data strictly confidential and shall not disclose it to any third party (other than authorised Sub-processors or as required by law) without the prior written consent of the Controller.

12. Liability

Each party's liability under this DPA shall be subject to the limitations set out in the applicable Terms of Service, except to the extent that applicable data-protection law imposes non-excludable liability. Nothing in this DPA limits liability for fraud, wilful misconduct, or gross negligence.

13. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

This DPA shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of England and Wales. The parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of England and Wales, without prejudice to the right of any supervisory authority to receive and investigate complaints.

14. Order of Precedence

In the event of conflict between this DPA and any other agreement between the parties, this DPA shall take precedence in respect of data-protection obligations. For all other matters, the Terms of Service shall take precedence.

Execution

This DPA is entered into by the parties as of the Effective Date stated in the Order Form or, if no Order Form exists, the date the Customer first accesses the Services.
